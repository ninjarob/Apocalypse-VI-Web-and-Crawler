# World Geography

## Overview

Apocalypse VI MUD features a vast, interconnected world spanning 74 distinct zones, from the bustling city of Midgaard to remote wilderness areas, ancient ruins, and mystical realms. The world is built on a coordinate-based system that allows for precise navigation and exploration.

### World Structure

The game world is organized around several key hubs and regions:

- **Midgaard**: The central hub city, divided into Northern and Southern sections connected by a bridge over the Candlebriar River
- **Major Cities**: Rome, Heliopolis, Juris, Raddok, and others serving as regional centers
- **Wilderness Areas**: Forests, mountains, deserts, and oceans connecting the populated areas
- **Special Zones**: Quest areas, dungeons, and unique locations with specific purposes

### Navigation System

The world uses a coordinate-based mapping system where:
- X coordinates increase eastward
- Y coordinates increase northward
- Each room has precise coordinates for accurate positioning
- Portal magic allows instant travel between key locations

## Zone Directory

### Central Hub - Midgaard (Zone 2)

**Difficulty**: 1 (Safe starting area)  
**Description**: The city of Midgaard is the center of economic trade, commerce, and travel throughout the imperial realm. Shops, guilds, inns and more line the various streets of the city, catering to citizens and adventurers alike.

**Key Areas**:
- **City Streets**: Central Street, Royal Boulevard, Wall Road, Park Road, Wizdale Avenue
- **Guild Halls**: All major class guilds are located within the city
- **Shops & Services**: General store, weapon shops, armor shops, magic shops
- **Government**: Town hall, mayor's office, city hall
- **Entertainment**: Grand Playhouse, battle arena, training grounds

**Level Ranges**: 1-40 (All classes welcome)

### Starting Areas

#### Training Grounds (Zone 5)
**Difficulty**: 1  
**Description**: The Training Grounds is an excellent place for adventurers to begin their quest towards becoming a legend within the realm.  
**Level Range**: 1-5  
**Recommended**: All classes

#### Midgaard Graveyard (Zone 3)
**Difficulty**: 1  
**Description**: Beyond Midgaard's southern gates lies the graveyard. For years the Gravekeeper has kept the place that honors Midgaard's fallen pristine. But now an evil lurks below the crypts, causing zombies to rise up to threaten and wreak havoc upon the beloved city.  
**Level Range**: 2-5  
**Recommended**: Good-aligned characters

#### Midgaard Sewers (Zone 4)
**Difficulty**: 2  
**Description**: Deep beneath the sleepless city of Midgaard lurks a world unseen by the eyes of most travelers. Here is where the thieves and murderers of Midgaard go to escape the eyes of the law, and where the rats which frequent the city by day and night call home.  
**Areas**: Tunnels (7-12), Pools (12-15)  
**Level Range**: 7-15

### Northern Wilderness

#### The Hills of Astyll (Zone 9)
**Difficulty**: 1  
**Description**: The Hills of Astyll blanket the land north of Midgaard. They provide a buffer between the peaceful city and the harsh mountains farther to the north and east. Beware the rampaging raiders that have taken their home in the Hills, and of the various creatures lurking within the cave.  
**Areas**: Grasslands (5-9), Cave (8-13)  
**Level Range**: 5-13

#### The Shire (Zone 10)
**Difficulty**: 2  
**Description**: The Shire is north of Midgaard, beyond the Hills of Astyll. Here is where the quiet halfling folk call their home. While the Thain maintains the Shire to extend safety to all travelers and adventurers alike, most residents would rather see the quaint landscape of their village remain unvisited by the outside world.  
**Areas**: General (5-11), Bree (10-15), Noldo Band (22-28), Bree Buildings (25-32)  
**Level Range**: 5-32

#### The Haunted Forest (Zone 12)
**Difficulty**: 2  
**Description**: Rumors run in numbers about the dark, oppressive woods to the East of Midgaard. Lumberjacks fear to enter, telling tales of a variety of wicked creatures that inhabit the dark woods.  
**Areas**: Woods (10-20), Treehouse (15-22)  
**Level Range**: 10-22

#### The Dwarven Kingdom (Zone 11)
**Difficulty**: 2-3  
**Description**: The ancient dwarven stronghold carved deep into the mountains north of the Shire.  
**Areas**: Main Castle (12-18), The Caverns (15-24), Mithril Hall (25-32)  
**Level Range**: 12-32

### Eastern Regions

#### The Great Eastern Desert (Zone 21)
**Difficulty**: 2  
**Description**: On the eastern side of the mountains, east of Midgaard, lies the Great Eastern Desert. Full of sand, dust, and scorpions, it is also rumoured to be home of the Darkside, a band of renegade mages.  
**Areas**: Desert (14-20), Fortress of Darkside (20-30)  
**Level Range**: 14-30

#### Heliopolis (Zone 20)
**Difficulty**: 3  
**Description**: Heliopolis, the Kingdom of the Sun, is an old city standing in the middle of the Great Eastern Desert. Travellers from far and wide make the difficult journey to Heliopolis to experience its unique culture.  
**Level Range**: 15-28

#### The Crypt of Nyarlathotep (Zone 22)
**Difficulty**: 5  
**Description**: Nyarlathotep was an old Pharaoh of Heliopolis who lived a wicked life. He was buried in a crypt to the north of Heliopolis, across the burning wastes. Before he died, He swore to return to Heliopolis and usurp the throne.  
**Level Range**: 25-36

#### Juris (Zone 47)
**Difficulty**: 3  
**Description**: Centuries ago, the city of Juris lay on a razor\'s edge between civilization and the vast, unexplored eastern frontier. After years of tragedy, the citizens established a system of justice unrivaled even in the imperial courts.  
**Level Range**: 18-28

#### Darkrime Mountain (Zone 23)
**Difficulty**: 4  
**Description**: To the north of the City of Juris lies Darkrime Mountain, a towering peak engulfed in an eternal white glacier. Its wind-ravaged peak is all but unassailable.

**Description**: To Forest of Haon-Dor may be found directly to the west of the City of Midgaard. Wolves, arachnids, and other foul creatures lurk beneath the thick canopy which prevents the sun from reaching the forest floor.

**Level Range**: 15-30

#### The Black Market (Zone 37)
**Difficulty**: 3  
**Description**: To the west of the Forest of Haon-Dor lies a den of thieves, pirates, and other men and women of ill repute. Within the walls of the coastal city, law and order ceases to exist.

#### Pirate's Stronghold (Zone 38)
**Difficulty**: 3  
**Description**: The first pirate attack on the village Schilling was directed toward their protector, the retired warrior, Keefe. With Keefe slain, his stronghold was claimed by the Dread Pirate.

#### The Great Ocean (Zone 7)
**Difficulty**: 1  
**Description**: Ages come and go, nations rise and fall, but the Great Ocean remains unchanged. Connecting the various port towns, rivers, and canals of the world, this vast body of water is of immense economic and strategic importance to the empire.  
**Level Range**: 1-40 (All classes)

## Terrain Types

The world features various terrain types that affect movement and combat:

- **Inside**: Urban areas, buildings, dungeons (safe, well-lit)
- **City**: Streets, roads, urban outdoor areas
- **Field**: Open grasslands, plains
- **Forest**: Wooded areas with trees and underbrush
- **Hills**: Rolling hills and elevated terrain
- **Mountain**: High peaks and rocky crags
- **Water**: Rivers, lakes, oceans
- **Swamp**: Marshy, muddy wetlands
- **Desert**: Sandy wastes and arid regions
- **Snow**: Frozen tundras and icy areas
- **Air**: Flying or levitating areas
- **Unknown**: Special or transitional areas

## Navigation Tips

### Getting Started
1. Begin in Midgaard (Zone 2) - the central hub city
2. Use the Training Grounds (Zone 5) for your first levels
3. Explore the immediate surroundings: Graveyard, Sewers, Hills of Astyll
4. Follow the main roads to other cities as you level

### Portal System
- **Portal Keys**: Each area has a unique portal key (combination of letters)
- **Portal Scrolls**: Allow instant travel to any known location
- **Portal Magic**: Advanced spell for experienced mages

### Level Progression Path
- **Levels 1-10**: Midgaard and immediate surroundings
- **Levels 10-20**: Northern hills, forests, and nearby cities
- **Levels 20-30**: Mountain ranges, deserts, and coastal areas
- **Levels 30-40**: High-level dungeons and remote wilderness
- **Levels 40+**: Legendary zones and special areas

### Transportation
- **Boats**: Available at the levee for river travel
- **Horses**: Can be purchased for faster overland travel
- **Flying**: Available to certain classes at higher levels
- **Portals**: Instant magical transportation
